Hey, welcome to our website. We built this to showcase our final project for Google's Applied Machine Learning Intensive (AMLI). Our project idea was to develop a website that would display real tweets next to fake ones and ask the user to guess the real one. We wanted to use machine learning to generate the text, and our first idea was to use a LSTM based neural network. Our results were mixed. We tried word level and character level modeling, but both produced subpar results. We were struggling with the development of our own models, so we looked to see if we were reinventing the wheel, we where!

In February 2019 the ML community saw a great advance in language modeling with Open ai's release of their gpt-2 117M model.Although this isn't the full model they used in their paper, the results are still impressive. Thanks to nsheppered's addtions to the original git repository, we are were able to train this state of the art model on our twitter data, producing some convincing fake tweets.

Producing fake tweets is nothing new, in our research we came across many examples, but the only one that really stuck out to us was DeepDrumpf created by @BradHayes. We were unable to speak with Mr. Hayes, so we don't know what he uses, but his results are impressive, his work was our original performance benchmark. All the examples we came across were utilizing a neural net with LSTM or GRU nodes. We believe this to be the first application fo GPT-2 117M to the tweet generation problem. Of course this is a cute application of some powerful technology, but we believe it to be an eye opening experiment with the possibilities of misinformation.
