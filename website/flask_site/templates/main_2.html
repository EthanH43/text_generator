Hey, welcome to our website. We built this to showcase our final project for Google's Applied Machine Learning Intensive (AMLI). Using cutting edge language modeling techniques we generate fake tweets and Shakespeare and ask you the user, Which one is real?

Producing fake tweets is nothing new, in our research we came across many examples, one particularly good example was DeepDrumpf created by @BradHayes. Most of the model structures were some variation of LSTM node Recurrent Neural Networks, but we hadn't seen any use of the gpt-2 model to generate tweets. We thought this might be due to it's relatively new release and we were happy to integrate what Open ai's has done so well into our application.

In February 2019 the ML community saw a great advance in language modeling with Openai's release of their gpt-2 117M model. They have kept the model used in the paper out of the public sphere, but the smaller model still produces some convincing results. We want to know what this means for the future of information?
